D'gamma Studios

Welcome to my studio! I've been building skills and my equipment collection for years. I got my start when I was about 14 years old. I mounted a speaker and battery powered amplifier into a backpack and started making beats on iMaschine on an iPod. After drumming for years at churches and Lake Forest Ranch, I started putting back money to get a little professional audio interface, a little midi keyboard, an Audio Technica AT2020 cardioid condenser microphone (this same model was used on a few Billie Eilish hits), and a pair of Audio Technica m50x studio headphones. I now have a 16-channel Presonus 16.4.2 soundboard, a Tascam US 16x08 audio interface, a custom modded tube mic (maybe similar to a C12, but also nothing sounds like a C12 except for a C12), almost a dozen other microphones, outboard gear, Presonus Eris E5 xt studio monitors, a desktop and laptop picked for processing speed to run my plethora of plugins with inaudible latency, and a lot more ear training and experience under my belt. Now that I feel like I can make music up to par with my expectations, I'm taking even bigger steps to grow my studio so I can find artists to help thrive in creativity.

First Ridgeland Christmas 2021

I had the honor of mixing the Christmas songs for First Ridgeland Baptist Church. We recorded everything like it was live, with about 3 takes for each song. We recorded half the songs on Monday, the other half on Wednesday, and I finished mixing by that Friday night so we could send it in to run on local TV! That was a very busy week.

First Ridgeland Christmas 2020

This was my first time mixing the Christmas songs for First Ridgeland Bapist Church. Again, we recorded like it was live on Monday and Wednesday, with about 3 takes per song. It was finished and turned in by that Saturday. I believe difference in quality between this and the next years production are solid testaments to the effort I put into honing my skills.
